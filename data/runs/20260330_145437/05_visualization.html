Finance Department Process Documentation & Flow Synthesis

Underground Gold Mining Operation — ~600 employees on site — Finance function: ~20 staff
Three detailed interviews (David Motswari, Priya Desai, James Kipchoge) reveal a finance function that is operationally effective due to individual competence and informal controls, but structurally fragile. Key findings: heavy reliance on spreadsheet workarounds bridging disconnected source systems (Ellipse, MineRP, Microster, Aspen), significant key-person dependencies concentrated in three individuals, and a recurring pattern of upstream coding errors that propagate uncorrected through the transaction chain. The function is well-positioned for targeted automation and process improvement.

6 Cross-Process Observations

⚙️ System Integration

No Integration Between Operational Systems and Ellipse ERP

MineRP (survey), Aspen (plant), Microster (time & attendance), and Deswik (planning) all feed into finance processes but none have live or automated integration with Ellipse. Every data transfer is manual — CSV files, flat-file extracts, or re-keying.

"It works because people fill the gap. That's the risk." — David Motswari
🔄 Handoff Friction

Goods Receipt Confirmation Bottleneck

2–3 day typical delay for operational staff to confirm goods receipts in Ellipse. This cascades into missed payment runs, vendor relationship strain, and Priya conducting informal verifications. Intended control path (via Themba) is routinely bypassed.

"There's no consequence for not raising a PO… so the behaviour doesn't change." — Priya Desai
📊 Data Quality

Upstream Coding Errors Propagate Uncorrected

Cost coding errors at requisition stage (performed by operational staff with no finance review) propagate through PO → goods receipt → invoice → ledger. Detection only occurs at month-end via manual review. 30–60 recode journals monthly = direct evidence of structural failure.

"The correction fixes the ledger. It doesn't fix the process." — James Kipchoge
👤 Key-Person Risk

Critical Knowledge Concentrated in Three Individuals

David's tracker (4 years on one laptop), Amara's allocation spreadsheet (sole maintainer), and Priya's compensating controls (undocumented) each represent single points of failure. No documented succession, no shared system access, no backup process for any of these.

"If I got hit by a truck tomorrow, they'd be in trouble. I know that." — David Motswari
⚙️ ERP Workflow Failure

Unreliable ERP Approval Notifications Force Parallel Process

Ellipse approval notifications are unreliable enough that Priya maintains a full parallel paper-based approval process — printing invoice packs, physically walking them to approvers, and filing signed copies. The ERP workflow exists but cannot be relied upon for deadline-critical processing.

"I can't rely on it alone if I have a deadline. So I have the parallel process as backup." — Priya Desai
📊 Labour Cost Risk

HR-to-Finance Mapping Table Has No Authoritative Version

Two teams (finance and HR) maintain separate copies of the cost centre mapping table. No formal protocol for updates on org changes. No import validation in Ellipse. If the mapping is wrong, labour costs post silently to incorrect cost centres. Labour is the largest single cost category.

All six cross-process observations are addressable with targeted interventions — several (structured journal narratives, mapping table governance, ERP notification fix) are low-cost and could be implemented quickly without system replacement.
