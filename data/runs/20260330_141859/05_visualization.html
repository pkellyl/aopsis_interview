Systems & Tools Landscape
Six platforms span all 14 process domains. No document management system is in use — the shared network drive serves this function informally.
Pronto (ERP)
Primary ERP system. Used for transactional processing, GL, fixed assets, depreciation, and cost centre reporting. Cost centre structure misaligned with operational reporting needs.
Accounts Payable GL / Journals Fixed Assets Depreciation Cost Centre Reporting Financial Reporting
Microsoft Excel
Pervasive across ALL planning, reporting, and accrual processes. Acts as the de-facto planning system, workflow tool, and control mechanism throughout the function. High fragility.
Weekly Scorecards Revenue Accrual Budget / Reforecast Management Pack Journal Log AP Exceptions CapEx Tracker Standing Data Close Calendar
Microsoft Word
Used for management pack narrative sections, weekly scorecard summaries (Dale), and business case documents. No live links to Excel data — all content is manually copy-pasted.
Management Reporting Weekly Scorecard Business Cases
Email (Outlook / Shared Mailbox)
Primary workflow and handoff mechanism across the entire function. apinvoices@site.com.au is the AP invoice intake channel. Close coordination, journal submissions, remittances, and group reporting all flow through email.
AP Invoice Receipt Journal Submissions Close Coordination Payment File Transfer Remittances Group Reporting
Online Banking Platform
Used exclusively by Graham Fettes for payment file upload and release. AP team prepares the file in Excel; Graham releases it. Single authoriser — no second approval required.
Payment Authorisation Cash Management
Shared Network Drive
Serves as the de-facto document management system. Used for AP invoice filing, audit working papers, Met team production tracking file, Kobus's standing data file, and budget models. No version control or access management.
AP Filing Audit Working Papers Budget Models Production Tracking Standing Data
The systems landscape is characterised by a single ERP (Pronto) that is structurally misaligned with operational needs, surrounded by an unmanaged layer of Excel files that carry the real intelligence of the function. This creates significant fragility, audit risk, and automation complexity.
