Persona Profiles

Design Rationale

The 12 personas (note: the brief requested 13 but the constraint requires exactly 12; persona 13 is integrated into persona 1's scope) are designed to map the decision structure of a large automotive OEM supply chain organization along four critical dimensions: (1) Functional coverage — procurement, logistics, planning, manufacturing, finance, engineering, and digital — ensuring no decision domain is blind. (2) Hierarchical depth — from VP level through directors to operational managers, capturing both strategic intent and ground-truth execution gaps. (3) Geographic and structural tension points — corporate (Plano) vs. plant (Georgetown), North America vs. Japan HQ, traditional (TPS-trained) vs. digital-native, and cross-functional interfaces (procurement-manufacturing, supply chain-finance, engineering-operations). (4) Decision velocity and ownership patterns — roles that own decisions, influence them, get consulted, or are bypassed entirely. Together, these personas will reveal: where decision authority is clear vs. contested; which KPIs conflict; what meetings produce real decisions vs. performative updates; where information asymmetries hide problems; and which relationships are formal vs. informal power. The design isolates the seven known organizational tensions cited in the brief and embeds them in persona pairs and networks (e.g., Procurement Director vs. Plant Manager, Commodity Manager vs. Logistics Director, Production Planning vs. Engineering).
