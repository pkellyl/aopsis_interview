Persona Profiles

Design Rationale

Five personas are selected to cover the most structurally distinct and information-rich positions in the finance function of this single-site underground gold mine. The selection balances on-site business partner versus central finance perspectives, long versus short tenure, and operational versus compliance/transactional orientations — ensuring the synthetic interview panel will surface diverse process knowledge, working styles, and pain points. Persona 1 — Underground Production Finance Business Partner: Represents the embedded operational finance perspective. Long-tenured, close to cost drivers, comfortable with informal workarounds. Critical for capturing cost accounting, business case development, and the site-vs-central tension from the site side. Will reveal how operational finance actually works versus how it is supposed to work. Persona 2 — Management Accountant (Central Finance): Mid-tenure central finance professional. Owns month-end close, management reporting, and cost allocation. Sits at the intersection of operational data from site partners and reporting outputs to management. Key source of process detail on close cycles, reporting packs, and reconciliation friction. Will surface handoff issues with business partners and data quality frustrations. Persona 3 — Accounts Payable Clerk: Junior, relatively new to site. High-volume transactional role covering invoice processing, PO matching, and vendor payments. Critical for capturing the AP process in granular step-by-step detail. Newer tenure means she is more likely to notice and articulate gaps, workarounds, and system limitations that longer-tenured staff have normalised. Also a proxy for automation sensitivity. Persona 4 — Finance Manager / Controller: Senior, long-tenured. Oversees the entire finance function, owns financial reporting, compliance, and year-end. Has both strategic and operational visibility. Key for understanding governance, approval flows, escalation paths, and the overall architecture of finance processes. Will provide cross-process perspective and surface key-person dependency risks they may not fully recognise in themselves. Persona 5 — Plant Processing Finance Business Partner: Represents the surface plant operational finance perspective — distinct from underground. Mid-tenure, technically oriented, close to production cost and ore processing economics. Important for capturing the revenue/cost interface (gold doré production), fixed assets in the plant context, and how the plant BP navigates between operational teams and central finance. Provides contrast to the underground BP persona and ensures plant-side processes are not collapsed into underground assumptions. Collectively these five personas cover: all major process domains in scope; both sides of the site-vs-central tension; junior, mid, and senior experience levels; short and long tenure; transactional, analytical, and strategic roles; and a spread of likely attitudes toward process documentation and automation.
