💻 Systems Landscape

Technology environment as described across all five interviews. Pronto (ERP) is the system of record; Excel is the de facto operating system for planning, reporting, and accruals.

Pronto (Primary ERP)

  • AP invoice entry & payment batch processing
  • Cost centre / GL reporting module
  • Fixed asset register & depreciation module
  • Payroll GL posting
  • Vendor master & bank details
  • PO management & goods receipting
⚠ Cost centre structure does not align to operational reporting needs — both BPs maintain Excel remapping layers to compensate.

Microsoft Excel (Pervasive)

  • Weekly scorecards (Dale, Kobus)
  • Management reporting pack assembly (Sarah)
  • Reforecast & budget consolidation (Graham)
  • Revenue & cost accrual calculations (Kobus)
  • Journal tracking log (Sarah)
  • AP Exceptions Tracker (Bree)
  • Capex tracker (Kobus)
  • Standing data file (Kobus)
  • Close coordination calendar (Sarah)
⚠ No version control, access management, or audit trail. Multiple files are single points of failure.

Microsoft Word

  • Management pack narrative sections
  • Business case documents
  • Weekly operational summary (Dale)
Excel tables and charts copy-pasted into Word; no live data links.

Online Banking Platform

  • Payment file upload (Bree prepares CSV/Excel)
  • Payment release authorisation (Graham — sole authoriser)
⚠ Single authoriser — no second approval before payment release.

Shared Network Drive

  • AP invoice archive (by vendor/month)
  • Audit working papers (Sarah)
  • Met team production tracking file
  • Kobus's standing data file
  • Graham's master budget/reforecast model
No formal document management system in use. No backup regime confirmed for critical files.

Email (Primary Workflow Channel)

  • Invoice receipt: apinvoices@[site].com.au
  • Journal submission from BPs to Sarah
  • Close calendar distribution
  • Business case and capex approval routing
  • Group finance recharge schedule delivery
  • Gold price & assay data from group/refinery
Email is the sole workflow management mechanism for many critical handoffs. No ticketing or document management system.
⚠ Key-Person Risk Summary

Seven distinct key-person dependencies were identified across five interviews. These represent processes that would be significantly impaired or halted if the relevant individual were absent without transition.

"Work has calcified around how I do it, not how it should be done." — Kobus van der Merwe, Business Partner — Plant Processing

Kobus van der Merwe — Revenue Accrual

Critical

The site's most material journal entry. Full calculation methodology (doré assay application, silver credit, treatment charge deductions, provisional estimate approach, reversal logic) exists only in Kobus's head and partially in his standing data file. No second person could execute this process without him.

Revenue Accrual Month-End Close Management Reporting

Kobus van der Merwe — Standing Data File

Critical

A single Excel file containing all cost centre mappings, contracted unit rates, and consumption rates that underpins the weekly scorecard, revenue accrual, cost accruals, and the entire plant budget build. No formal backup, no second maintainer, no documentation.

All Plant Processes Cost Accounting Budgeting

Graham Fettes — Reforecast Consolidation Model

Critical

Undocumented master Excel model. Internal consistency checks rely entirely on Graham's 9 years of operational memory. Some forecast numbers represent his interpretation of BP narrative submissions — assumption ownership is ambiguous. Risk registered; mitigation not executed.

Budgeting & Forecasting Management Reporting

Sarah Okonkwo — Depreciation & Close Management

High

Sole operator of Pronto's depreciation module with no documentation of its configuration. The month-end close sequencing, task dependencies, and exception handling logic exists in Sarah's memory, with the Excel calendar as the only partial external record.

Dale Hutchinson — Scorecard Remapping Layer

High

The SUMIF/INDEX-MATCH cost centre remapping logic in Dale's scorecard has been built up over 14 years and is undocumented. No other person maintains or fully understands the lookup table structure. Loss of this file would break weekly and month-end underground production reporting.

Graham Fettes — Sole Payment Authoriser

High

Graham is the only authoriser on the online banking platform. No second approval is required before payments are released. His review depth varies with workload — explicitly acknowledged as less thorough during close week. Constitutes both a key-person risk and a financial control gap.

Bree Callahan — AP Exceptions Tracker

Medium

Bree maintains a parallel AP Exceptions Tracker (Excel) because Pronto's status field does not support exception workflow tracking. If the spreadsheet is lost or falls behind, all exception visibility is lost. No second person understands the full state of outstanding AP exceptions.

🔐 Internal Control Observations

Control weaknesses identified incidentally during process documentation interviews. These are not the result of a formal internal controls review.

  • #10 No segregation of duties — AP non-PO invoice approval
    Bree performs both invoice keying and approval status-setting for non-PO invoices. Graham's payment file review is a spot-check, not line-by-line. Affected: Accounts Payable · Nature: Control gap
  • #11 Single payment authoriser on banking platform
    Graham is sole authoriser; no second approval required. Volume-dependent review depth, acknowledged by Graham as a residual risk. Affected: Cash Management · Nature: Key-person risk / Control gap
  • + Invoice back-dating without escalation (Bree's observation)
    Invoices discovered during vendor statement reconciliation are back-dated and posted without escalation to Sarah or Graham unless material. Correctness of this approach uncertain. Affected: Accounts Payable / Month-End · Nature: Workaround / Control gap
  • + No formal invoice receipt log
    No formal log exists at point of invoice receipt. Network folder and Bree's memory are the safety net. Unprocessed invoices could sit unnoticed. Affected: Accounts Payable · Nature: Workaround / Control gap

High-signal opportunities for RPA, workflow automation, or agentic AI, derived from cross-process observations. Prioritised by effort, frequency, and value impact.

Opportunity Process Type Priority Rationale
Automated remittance advice generation & distribution Accounts Payable RPA High ~100 hrs/year; zero judgement required; vendor email data fragmentation solvable with master contact list. Highest effort-to-value RPA candidate.
Standard journal template + automated completeness check Month-End Close Workflow High 30–40% of journals returned for rework; structured template + validation rules could eliminate a full day of close delay with minimal cost.
Automated management reporting pack assembly Management Reporting RPA High Data extraction → Excel formatting → Word assembly is fully rules-based and repeatable monthly. Live Pronto-to-report pipeline eliminates manual correction chain.
Revenue accrual calculation engine (agentic) AR & Revenue Agentic AI High Multi-input calculation (production data, assay, gold price, treatment charges) with provisional fallback logic. High value, undocumented, critical concentration risk. AI agent could execute calculation, apply provisional rules, prepare journal, flag variances, and manage reversal cycle.
AP three-way matching & exception routing automation Accounts Payable RPA High Invoice receipt → Pronto keying → PO match → exception flagging → routing is rules-based at ~60–90 invoices/week. Automation removes manual keying, eliminates AP Exceptions Tracker spreadsheet dependency, and enforces SOD.
Budget/reforecast submission consolidation & consistency checking Budgeting & Forecasting Agentic AI Medium Graham spends 20–30% of reforecast cycle on stakeholder engagement to secure quality inputs. An AI agent could validate submissions against historical actuals, flag outliers, translate narrative to structured numbers, and surface inconsistencies before Graham's review.
Depreciation run automation with configuration documentation Fixed Assets & CapEx RPA Medium First step is documenting Sarah's depreciation module configuration (eliminates key-person risk). Then automation of monthly depreciation run, reconciliation, and journal posting is straightforward.
Pronto cost centre structure realignment + automated remapping Cost Accounting Workflow Medium Root-cause fix for both BPs' Excel remapping layers. Realigning Pronto cost centres to operational reporting needs removes the weekly manual remap step and eliminates the structural debt in multiple spreadsheet models.
Capex approval workflow with document repository Fixed Assets & CapEx Workflow Medium Currently entirely email-based with no audit trail. A lightweight workflow tool could route business cases, capture approvals, store versions, and link to Pronto asset records — eliminating email-inbox dependency.
Weekly scorecard automation (data pull + format) Cost Accounting / Reporting RPA Lower Both BPs perform identical Monday-morning Pronto pull → paste → remap cycles. Automation of the data extraction and remapping step is straightforward once cost centre structure is resolved or the lookup tables are formalised.
