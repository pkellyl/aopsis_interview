Process Documentation and Flow Synthesis Report — Finance Function, Single-Site Underground Gold Mining Operation

Executive Summary

This report consolidates findings from five structured interviews conducted across the finance function of a single-site underground gold mining operation, covering all 14 defined process domains and spanning both on-site business partner and central finance roles. The finance function operates as a split team: two embedded business partners (underground production and plant processing) supported by a central team responsible for transactional processing, close management, reporting, compliance, and planning. Process maturity is highly variable — transactional processes are well-structured but control-light, while planning, forecasting, and specialist accrual processes carry significant undocumented complexity and concentrated key-person dependency. This report is intended to provide the evidential base for subsequent pain point mapping and identification of RPA, workflow automation, and agentic AI opportunities.

Section 1: Finance Function Overview

## Finance Function Overview

Team Structure

The finance function operates across two distinct structural layers: **on-site business partners** embedded in operational departments, and a **central finance team** handling transactional, reporting, compliance, and planning processes. Five interviews were conducted, representing four roles across both layers.

Systems Landscape

  • **Pronto** — Primary ERP system. Used across AP (invoice entry, payment batch processing), cost centre reporting, fixed asset register, depreciation module, payroll GL posting, and financial reporting. Multiple interviewees noted that Pronto's cost centre structure does not align with operational reporting needs, requiring manual remapping workarounds.
  • **Microsoft Excel** — Pervasive across all planning, reporting, and accrual processes. Serves as the primary tool for weekly scorecards (Dale, Kobus), management reporting pack assembly (Sarah), reforecast and budget models (Graham), revenue and cost accrual calculations (Kobus), close coordination calendar (Sarah), and journal tracking (Sarah, Bree).
  • **Microsoft Word** — Used for management pack narrative sections and business case documents.
  • **Online Banking Platform** — Used by Graham exclusively for payment file upload and authorisation; AP team prepares the file, Graham releases it.
  • **Shared Network Drive** — Used for audit working papers, AP invoice filing, the Met team's production tracking file, and Kobus's standing data file. No document management system is in use.
  • **Email (shared AP mailbox: apinvoices@[site].com.au)** — Primary invoice receipt channel; also the main communication mechanism for close coordination, journal submission, and business case routing.

    ## Process Domain Summaries — Transactional Processes

    Accounts Payable

    **Roles:** Bree Callahan (AP Clerk — primary owner); Graham Fettes (payment authorisation); Procurement team (PO management, exception resolution)

    **Trigger:** Invoice receipt via shared AP mailbox (apinvoices@[site].com.au), occasional paper delivery.

    **End-to-End Flow:**

    1. Bree receives invoice (PDF standard; occasionally Excel/Word), performs validity check (invoice number, vendor, amount, GST, date present), downloads and files to network folder by vendor/month.

    2. Invoice keyed into Pronto AP module: vendor code, invoice number, date, amount, GST, description.

    3. **PO-matched invoices:** PO number looked up in Pronto; three-way match performed (PO quantity/price vs. invoice vs. goods receipt). Tolerances: minor quantity/price variances accepted with notation; material variances or fully-consumed POs flagged in the **AP Exceptions Tracker spreadsheet** and chased with procurement.

    4. **Non-PO invoices:** Keyed in pending state; Bree emails relevant cost centre owner for coding approval. No formal owner directory — judgement-based routing.

    5. Clean-matched or approved invoices set to 'approved/ready for payment' status by Bree. *Note: Bree performs both keying and approval — no segregation of duties on non-PO invoices.*

    6. **Payment runs** (Tuesday and Thursday mornings): Bree pulls 'approved + due' invoice list from Pronto, performs sanity check, generates bank payment file (CSV/Excel), emails to Graham with total value and exception notes.

    7. Graham reviews file, logs into online banking platform, uploads file, and releases payment. Single authoriser — no second approver.

    8. Bree manually composes and sends remittance advices to vendors by email (~1 hour per run). Vendor email addresses sourced from Pronto contacts field (incomplete) plus Bree's personal contact list.

    **Frequency:** Invoice processing daily; payment runs twice weekly (Tue/Thu); volume ~60–90 invoices per week.

    **Key decision points:** PO match tolerances (Bree's judgement); non-PO coding approval (cost centre owner); payment file review (Graham).

    **Systems:** Pronto AP module, AP Exceptions Tracker (Excel), email, online banking platform.

    Accounts Receivable and Revenue

    *Covered in depth under Kobus van der Merwe's process — see Section 4 (Specialist Processes) for the revenue accrual detail, as this site generates revenue through a single gold doré stream rather than a traditional AR/invoicing process. No separate AR or customer invoicing function was identified.*

    Payroll and Labour Cost Processing

    **Roles:** Payroll Administrator (payroll run — not interviewed); Sarah Okonkwo (GL posting); Dale Hutchinson (accrual estimation at month-end)

    **Trigger:** Payroll Administrator processes monthly payroll in first few days of new month (e.g., January payroll processed in early February). Sends summary to Sarah by email.

    **End-to-End Flow (GL side):**

    1. Payroll Administrator completes payroll run; emails Excel summary to Sarah showing gross pay by cost centre, PAYG tax, superannuation, and one-off adjustments.

    2. Sarah posts three journals in Pronto: (a) payroll expense by cost centre (P&L), (b) payroll liabilities (balance sheet — tax, super payable), (c) net pay clearing entry.

    3. Sarah reconciles payroll liability accounts as part of monthly balance sheet reconciliation cycle.

    4. Dale uses contractor timesheet data independently to estimate labour accruals for production cost centres at month-end (parallel, informal).

    **Frequency:** Monthly.

    **Coverage note:** The payroll run itself (Payroll Administrator's process) was not directly captured — only the downstream GL posting step. Key inputs, systems used by the Payroll Administrator, and any exception handling in payroll processing are not documented.

    ---

    Procurement and PO Management

    **Roles:** Procurement team (PO creation — not interviewed); Bree Callahan (PO matching in AP); Graham Fettes (escalation on consumed POs)

    **Trigger:** Operational request for goods or services triggers PO creation by the procurement team in Pronto.

    **End-to-End Flow (finance interface):**

    1. PO created in Pronto by procurement; goods receipt recorded on delivery.

    2. Bree matches vendor invoice to PO and goods receipt at point of AP processing (see Accounts Payable above).

    3. Discrepancies (quantity, price, consumed PO) escalated to procurement for PO amendment or new PO creation.

    4. New vendor setup: Bree completes vendor setup form, collects ABN and bank details (written confirmation required for bank details — fraud risk protocol); submits to Systems Admin for Pronto vendor record creation. Bank detail changes follow same written-confirmation protocol.

    **Coverage note:** The procurement team's PO creation and goods receipting process was not directly interviewed. Finance visibility into procurement is primarily through the AP matching interface.

    Section 3: Process Domain Summaries — Reporting and Planning Processes

    ## Process Domain Summaries — Reporting and Planning Processes

    Cost Accounting and Allocation

    **Roles:** Dale Hutchinson (underground production cost centres); Kobus van der Merwe (plant processing cost centres); Sarah Okonkwo (intercompany recharge journals, allocation postings)

    **Key characteristic:** Both business partners operate Excel-based remapping layers between Pronto's cost centre structure and their operational reporting structures. Pronto cost centres reflect a historical GL design that does not align with current operational reporting needs (stoping areas, development groups, plant circuits). Both models use lookup tables in Excel (Dale's in his scorecard; Kobus's in a standalone **standing data file**) to remap GL/cost centre codes into operationally meaningful categories.

    **Weekly rhythm (both BPs):** Monday morning Pronto actuals pull → paste into Excel model → remapping via VLOOKUP/SUMIF against lookup tables → variance vs. phased budget → one-page summary to respective operational manager before 9–10 AM.

    **Month-end:** Full month actuals pulled; accruals added; complete variance pack prepared for management reporting.

    **Intercompany recharges (Sarah):** Group finance sends a monthly schedule (~25th of month) listing management fees and shared service recharges. Sarah validates against prior month and annual schedule, queries anomalies, and posts journal in Pronto.

    ---

    Management Reporting

    **Roles:** Sarah Okonkwo (pack preparation); Graham Fettes (review, narrative, group dashboard); Dale/Kobus (variance commentary input); Mine Manager (recipient)

    **Trigger:** Sarah's close calendar; pack due to Graham by day 3 of close cycle.

    **End-to-End Flow:**

    1. Sarah pulls P&L and cost centre reports from Pronto reporting module (saved reports); exports to Excel.

    2. Formats in Excel: adds budget vs. actual columns, calculates variances. Budget data manually maintained in Excel alongside Pronto data.

    3. Business partners (Dale, Kobus) submit variance commentary — due day 2 to Sarah. In practice, timing is variable; Graham sometimes does bilateral follow-up on day 4.

    4. Sarah assembles 20–25 page pack: Word document with embedded Excel tables and charts (copy-paste from Excel; no live links). Includes P&L, balance sheet summary, cash flow, cost centre schedules, production metrics, capex tracking, headcount/labour, and variance commentary.

    5. Sarah emails pack to Graham (Excel/Word file attachment) by day 3–4.

    6. Graham reviews; writes management commentary at front of pack; signs off; Sarah converts to PDF for Mine Manager.

    7. Graham presents at management meeting (day 7); Mine Manager is recipient.

    8. Graham separately prepares **Group CFO dashboard** (one page, Excel, group-prescribed template) from finalised pack numbers — built on day 6, emailed to Group CFO by end of day 6.

    **Timing:** Monthly; 5–6 working day close window. Group dashboard due day 6.

    **Systems:** Pronto (reporting module), Excel, Word, email.

    **Notable risk:** Saved Pronto reports were set up years ago and have not been verified against the current cost centre structure (Sarah's observation).

    ---

    Budgeting and Forecasting

    **Roles:** Graham Fettes (coordination, consolidation, challenge, Group submission); Dale Hutchinson (underground production budget); Kobus van der Merwe (plant processing budget); Budget Analyst (coordination support — not interviewed)

    **Annual Budget (Oct–Nov):**

  • Triggered by group budget cycle (~August, 12 weeks' notice for site submission).
  • BPs build departmental budgets with respective operational managers: activity-based (development metres, tonnes, equipment utilisation for Dale; tonnes milled, head grade, recovery, plant cost build-up for Kobus).
  • Phased weekly/monthly — not flat annual ÷ 12.
  • Submitted to Graham via Budget Analyst coordination; Graham consolidates in a **master Excel reforecast/budget model** (sits on shared drive, Graham-maintained).
  • Graham reviews for internal consistency (knowledge-based, not systematised); pushes back on BPs for narrative support.
  • Submitted to group; loaded into Pronto after approval.
  • BPs manually transcribe approved budget into their own scorecard models (no automated feed from Pronto).

    • **Quarterly Reforecast:**

  • Triggered by Graham setting timeline; BPs submit updated forecasts using a standardised Excel template.
  • Quality variable — Graham describes spending 20–30% of reforecast time on stakeholder engagement to secure quality inputs.
  • Some narrative submissions translated to numbers by Graham or Sarah (ownership of resulting assumptions is ambiguous).
  • Consolidated in Graham's master model; no structural reforecast process documented for mid-year operational changes (handled ad hoc by BPs in separate models).

    • ---

    Month-End and Year-End Close

    **Roles:** Sarah Okonkwo (close owner/coordinator); Graham Fettes (governance, review, sign-off); Dale Hutchinson, Kobus van der Merwe (journal/accrual submission); Payroll Administrator (payroll summary submission); Bree Callahan (AP support — accrual invoice identification)

    **Month-End Trigger:** ~Day 25, Sarah circulates close calendar (Excel file on shared drive) with task list, owners, and deadlines.

    **Close Sequence (5-day window post-month-end):**

  • **Day 0 (month-end):** Sarah pulls trial balance from Pronto — sanity check for obvious anomalies.
  • **Day 1:** Sarah chases business partners (Dale, Kobus, Engineering) for month-end journal submissions by CoB. Journals arrive by email with Excel attachments — no standard template. Sarah manually reviews each for completeness (GL account, cost centre, amount, description, period). Incomplete journals (estimated 30–40% of submissions) returned for rework — can consume a full day of delay.
  • **Day 1–2:** Sarah posts complete journals in Pronto. Maintains **journal tracking log** (Excel) with receipt date, BP name, description, amount, GL accounts, completeness status, posting date, and notes. This log is the only workflow tracking mechanism; Pronto records postings but not workflow.
  • **Day 2–3:** Sarah runs **depreciation** in Pronto (reviews fixed asset register for new/disposed assets, checks rates, runs module, posts journal, reconciles charge to accumulated depreciation movement). Kobus submits **revenue accrual** journal; Sarah posts.
  • **Day 3:** Sarah finalises trial balance, completes **balance sheet reconciliations** (~30 accounts, categorised as straightforward/cash-type, dependency-heavy/accruals, and complex/estimation-based). Intercompany recharge journal posted. Draft management pack sent to Graham.
  • **Day 3–4:** Graham reviews pack; bilateral follow-up on outstanding variance commentary.
  • **Day 4–5:** Sarah incorporates Graham's commentary; finalises pack as PDF.
  • **Day 5:** Close deadline — books locked. Management pack to Mine Manager.
  • **Day 6:** Graham prepares and submits Group CFO dashboard.

    • **Year-End (Dec–Feb):** Overlaps with audit. Additional complexity: full annual leave provision, impairment reviews, fair value adjustments, extended accruals. Audit fieldwork October–November; signed accounts targeted by end of November (calendar year-end). Sarah prepares audit working papers (Excel workbooks in shared drive subfolder structure; auditors access directly). Graham manages audit relationship, materiality, and accounting treatment negotiations.

    "It's like doing two close cycles at the same time except one of them is much more detailed." — Sarah Okonkwo, on year-end close

    Section 4: Process Domain Summaries — Specialist and Operational Finance Processes

    ## Process Domain Summaries — Specialist and Operational Finance Processes

    Fixed Assets and CapEx

    **Roles:** Kobus van der Merwe (capex tracking, monthly reconciliation with fixed asset register); Sarah Okonkwo (depreciation, fixed asset register maintenance in Pronto); Graham Fettes (capex approval gate ≥$100k; business case sign-off ≥$50k)

    **CapEx approval flow:** Business case originated by operational manager or BP → Graham endorsement (site gate) → Mine Manager approval → Group finance final sign-off. No centralised document repository; approval trail lives in email inboxes. Graham reviews payback/NPV, cost reasonableness, operational rationale.

    **Kobus's capex-to-asset reconciliation (monthly, ~2–3 hours):** Kobus maintains a plant-specific capex tracker (Excel) showing approved projects, committed costs, and expected capitalisation dates. At month-end, he reconciles this against Pronto's fixed asset register to confirm that capitalised items match what he expects. Discrepancies investigated and resolved with Sarah. Critical control — asset register drift identified as a specific risk.

    **Depreciation (Sarah, monthly):** Full detail in Section 3 (Month-End Close).

    ---

    Tax and Statutory Compliance

    **Roles:** Graham Fettes (oversight, sign-off, external adviser relationship); Sarah Okonkwo (data preparation, compliance report preparation)

    Sarah prepares the monthly compliance report (BAS/GST and similar) from Pronto data and submits to Graham for sign-off. Graham reviews at judgment level — not line-by-line verification. Annual tax return managed through external advisers; Graham coordinates, provides financials, reviews before lodgement. Sarah prepares supporting schedules. Statutory accounts prepared by Sarah (drafting) and Graham (review/sign-off), aligned to audit completion.

    **Coverage note:** This domain had relatively thin interview coverage. The detailed mechanics of BAS preparation, tax provision calculations, and statutory account preparation were not fully explored.

    ---

    Business Partnering and Business Case Support

    **Roles:** Dale Hutchinson (underground production); Kobus van der Merwe (plant processing); Graham Fettes (approval gate and quality review)

    Both BPs perform ad hoc cost modelling, budget tracking, and variance analysis for their operational managers throughout the month. Business cases (equipment, contractors, operational spend ≥$50k) are built by the relevant BP in Excel (NPV, payback, cost-benefit), reviewed by the operational manager, submitted to Graham for endorsement, then to the Mine Manager. 4–6 per year for underground; frequency varies for plant. No standard business case template across the group.

    ---

    Inventory and Stores Finance Interface

    **Coverage note:** This domain was not meaningfully covered across any of the five interviews. References to consumable inventory (reagents, materials) appeared in the context of Kobus's accrual calculations (consumption data from plant systems) and Bree's AP processing (spare parts invoices). No interviewee described a formal inventory finance reconciliation process or a stores/warehouse finance interface role. This domain should be investigated further.

    ---

    Cash Management (Site-Level)

    **Roles:** Graham Fettes (payment release — sole authoriser on banking platform); Bree Callahan (payment file preparation); Sarah Okonkwo (bank reconciliation as part of balance sheet recs)

    Site-level cash management is effectively the payment run process (described in Section 2 under Accounts Payable) plus Sarah's monthly bank reconciliation (Pronto cash balance vs. bank statement, timing differences identified). No separate treasury or cash forecasting function was described at site level. Petty cash reconciliation also sits with Sarah.

    ---

    Intercompany and Recharges

    **Roles:** Sarah Okonkwo (validation and posting); Graham Fettes (escalation for disputes)

    Group finance sends a monthly recharge schedule to Sarah (~25th of month) by email (Excel spreadsheet). Sarah validates: (1) total reasonableness vs. prior month; (2) line items vs. annual agreed schedule; (3) GL account coding correctness. Queries raised directly with group finance contact. Once validated, Sarah posts the recharge journal in Pronto. Matching intercompany account confirmed as nil balance before close. If group finance cannot resolve a query before close deadline, Sarah posts based on the schedule and flags the discrepancy for next month.
